15-Min Full-Body Dumbbell Routine

Simple, repeatable, and designed for muscle growth, strength, and an aesthetic physique using just your 5 kg dumbbells. Run the same session whenever you can — consistency beats complexity.

Equipment: Pair of 5 kg PVC dumbbells • Duration: 15 minutes

Structure Overview

  • Prep (3 min) — Mobility & activation
  • Strength Circuit (10 min) — 3 rounds
  • Finisher (2 min) — Burnout to pump & core

Prep & Activation  3 min

Move continuously. Spend about 30 seconds per drill. Focus on range of motion and breathing.

  1. Bodyweight squats — sit deep, drive knees out
  2. Cat-cow spine waves on all fours
  3. World’s greatest stretch (alternating sides)
  4. Arm circles with scap squeeze (forward/back)
  5. Marching high-knees with core braced
  6. Band pull-aparts substitute: floor snow angels

Core Strength Circuit  10 min

Complete the 6 moves in order, rest only 15–20 seconds between exercises. Aim for 3 rounds. Take a 30-second sip break between rounds if needed.

1. Goblet Squat

10–12 reps

Hold both dumbbells at chest. Sit deep, drive through heels, squeeze glutes at the top.

2. Staggered RDL

8/side

Front leg soft bend, back leg toes for balance. Hinge at hips, feel stretch in hamstrings, drive hips forward.

3. Bent-Over Row

12 reps

Neutral spine, pull elbows toward ribcage, pause 1 sec at top.

4. Standing Shoulder Press

10 reps

Brace glutes and core. Press overhead with a slight arc, control the descent.

5. Reverse Lunge Curl

8/side

Step back into lunge while curling. Keep elbows close and torso vertical.

6. Half-Kneeling Chop

10/side

Kneel with front foot planted. Hold both bells together, chop diagonally across body to engage core.

Finisher  2 min

Push hard but keep form sharp. Option: go back-to-back twice if you have energy.

  • 45 sec — Dumbbell dead-stop push-up rows (push-up on dumbbells, row at top alternating arms)
  • 45 sec — Suitcase march (hold bells at sides, slow marching steps, ribs down)
  • 30 sec — Hollow body flutter kicks with one dumbbell overhead

Progression & Consistency Tips

Weekly Rhythm

Aim for 3 sessions/week. If you miss days, just pick up where you left off. Plan rest days after two back-to-back sessions.

  • Mon
  • Tue
  • Wed
  • Thu
  • Fri
  • Sat
  • Sun

How to Progress

  • Move with intent — focus on slow lowers and strong finishes
  • Add reps: hit the top of the range before adding an extra round
  • Reduce rest: trim 5 seconds from transitions to raise intensity
  • For variety, swap the finisher for 2 minutes of farmer carries pacing your room

Optional Cooldown & Recovery

Spend 2–3 minutes on slow nasal breathing, child’s pose, and light quad/hamstring stretches. Hydrate and get a protein-rich meal within an hour.

Daily Setup Checklist

Effort Scale

Rate each session from 1 (easy) to 5 (max effort). Aim for mostly 3–4. If you string two 5s together, extend rest between rounds next time.
